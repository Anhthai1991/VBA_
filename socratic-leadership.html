Athens, 470 BC

Expose hidden assumptions through progressive questioning. Never accept the first answer. Dig until the real belief surfaces.

✓ Best for: Team debates, process challenges, when someone is sure they're right but you're not

Coaching, 1980s · Whitmore

Deep Dive: The 7 Powerful Questions (Stanier)

Michael Bungay Stanier's research found these 7 questions unlock 90% of workplace conversations. Memorize these first.

① The Kickstart Question
"What's on your mind?"

Opens space. Better than "How are you?" — it signals you're ready for real talk. Use to start every 1-on-1.

② The AWE Question
"And what else?"

The first answer is rarely the real answer. This one question triples what you learn. Use it 2–3 times per conversation.

③ The Focus Question
"What's the real challenge here for you?"

Cuts through noise. "For you" personalizes it — the challenge they own, not just an abstract problem.

④ The Foundation Question
"What do you want?"

Shockingly underused. Forces clarity. Most people haven't articulated what they actually want — this unlocks it.

⑤ The Lazy Question
"How can I help?"

Don't assume what help looks like. This stops you from solving problems that weren't yours to solve.

⑥ The Strategic Question
"If you're saying yes to this, what are you saying no to?"

Forces prioritization thinking. Critical when your team is overloaded or taking on too many projects.

⑦ The Learning Question
"What was most useful for you?"

Ends every meeting by reinforcing insight. Shifts ownership of learning back to them, not you.

Your 12-Week
Learning Roadmap

Structured for a busy Warehouse Manager — 15–20 min/day

Weeks 1–2 · Foundation

Understand Why Questions Are More Powerful Than Answers

  • 📖 Read: The Coaching Habit — Stanier (core text, 200 pages, practical)
  • 🎯 Practice: Replace 3 daily directives with questions. Log the results.
  • 🧠 Study: The Socratic Method — what it is, what it isn't
  • ✍️ Exercise: Write down your top 5 most-used phrases as a manager. Turn each into a question.
  • ⚡ Quick win: Start every morning standup with "What's on your mind today?"
Weeks 3–4 · The 5 Whys Mastery

Root Cause Questioning for Operations

  • 📖 Study: Toyota Production System — 5 Whys methodology
  • 🎯 Apply: Take 1 real WH incident per week. Run a full 5-Why analysis with your team, out loud
  • 🧠 Key skill: Learning to hold back your answer while waiting for their 5th Why
  • ✍️ Exercise: Document 3 recurring problems at Tessellation. Map root causes using 5 Whys.
  • ⚡ Combine with: "And what else?" after each Why answer
Weeks 5–7 · GROW Coaching Model

Structured 1-on-1 Conversations That Develop Your Team

  • 📖 Read: Coaching for Performance — Sir John Whitmore (20 pages on GROW is enough)
  • 🎯 Practice: Run one 15-min GROW session per week with a direct report
  • 🧠 Master: The sequence — Goal → Reality → Options → Will (never skip steps)
  • ✍️ Exercise: Prepare 4 GROW questions for your next performance conversation
  • ⚡ WH Context: Use GROW when a team lead is struggling with a recurring problem — let them find their own solution
Weeks 8–9 · Socratic Dialogue

Challenge Assumptions Without Challenging People

  • 📖 Study: Socratic Method core principles — elenchus, maieutics, aporia
  • 🎯 Practice: When you disagree with a proposal, ask 3 questions before stating your view
  • 🧠 Key skill: The "what do you mean by..." question — forces precise thinking
  • ✍️ Exercise: Role-play a vendor negotiation using only questions for the first 5 minutes
  • ⚡ Warning: This can feel confrontational — pair it with appreciative framing
Weeks 10–11 · Appreciative Inquiry

Build Culture by Asking About Strengths, Not Failures

  • 📖 Study: Appreciative Inquiry — Cooperrider's 4-D model (Discover, Dream, Design, Destiny)
  • 🎯 Practice: In your next team meeting, open with "Tell me about a time this team solved something really well"
  • 🧠 Key insight: Problems are social constructs — what we pay attention to grows
  • ✍️ Exercise: Design 3 "best-of" questions for your Tessellation team onboarding
  • ⚡ Apply: When morale is low after a bad day, shift to Appreciative questions immediately
Week 12 · Integration & Personal Style

Develop YOUR Question Signature

  • 🎯 Build your personal "Question Library" — 20 go-to questions organized by situation
  • 🧠 Reflect: Which framework feels most natural? Which is hardest? Why?
  • ✍️ Final exercise: Record yourself in a real conversation. Count questions vs. statements.
  • 📊 Target ratio: 60% questions / 40% statements in leadership conversations
  • ⚡ Next step: Teach these frameworks to your team leads — teaching is the deepest learning

The One Habit to Start TODAY

The "Hold 5 Seconds" Rule

After asking a question — say nothing for 5 seconds. This is harder than it sounds. The discomfort you feel is the same discomfort your team member uses to generate a real answer. Silence is not awkward — it's thinking space. Most managers jump in and kill the insight before it surfaces.

Practice this tomorrow morning. Time yourself. 5 seconds feels like 30.

Advanced: The Question Audit

Once a Week — Record Yourself

Use your phone to record a 10-minute meeting (with team's knowledge). Count:

  • Number of questions asked
  • Number of statements / directives
  • Average time between question and your next speaking turn

Target ratio: 60% questions / 40% statements. Most managers start at 10% / 90%. The data doesn't lie.

The Questions That Kill Conversations — Avoid These

❌ Closed questions

"Did you finish the report?" → Yes/no. Dead end. Use: "How is the report progressing?"

❌ Leading questions

"Don't you think we should X?" → You already answered it. Use: "What options do you see?"

❌ Multi-part questions

"What happened, why, and what will you do?" → Overwhelms. Ask one at a time.

❌ Why-accusations

"Why did you do it that way?" → Sounds like blame. Use: "Walk me through your thinking."
